Scotland Of India

Coorg

Coorg, officially called Kodagu, is the most sought after and popular hill station of Karnataka.The best time to visit Kodagu is between October to May and the peak season for this hill station is within February to May. Kodagu is the largest producer of Coffee in India. Also, it is one of the places with highest rainfall across the nation. Undulating hills covered in lush green forests and a landscape dotted with coffee plantations, tea gardens and orange groves, this hill station has breathtakingly stunning scenic beauty.

Coorg:Places To Visit

1. Talacauvery Temple & Waterfalls

Talacauvery Temple & Waterfalls

alacauvery is a destination of spiritual importance located at a distance of 44 KM from Madikeri, Coorg. Nestled in the Brahmagiri Hills, Talacauvery is the birthplace of the holy river Cauvery and is one of the most popular sightseeing places to see in Coorg. The water of the tank is believed to relieve a person of his sins. The best time to visit Talacauvery is during the pious festival of Tala Sakarmana in October. This is a destination for Hindu devotees and photography enthusiasts.

2. Abbey Falls

Abbey Falls

Abbey Falls or Abbi Falls is one of the most significant waterfalls not only in Coorg but the entire Western Ghats! To reach the falls, you will have to trek a short distance that will take you three stocky coffee bushes and aromatic spice plantations! A hanging bridge and a small balcony have been constructed for tourists to safely view the falls. End your visit here by sipping on some piping hot coffee at the stores nearby!

3. Madikeri Fort

Madikeri Fort

Madikeri Fort and Palace along with being the most important tourist spot in Madikeri is also a building that functions as government offices, Court and the district prison.The must-see attractions within the fort is the palace, Mahatma Gandhi Public Library, Maha Kote Ganesha temple, Clock Tower, Government Museum and the life-size statues of two elephants. There are also some viewpoints along the boundaries of Madikeri Fort.
